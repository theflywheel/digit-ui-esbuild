Nairobi’s biggest component-level breaks from the DIGIT theme sit in the action and navigation shell, not just the palette. The primary CTA switches from DIGIT orange/white Roboto to a 40px yellow/black Inter button, the header moves from blue to dark green with a pale-green back strip, and complaint tags become larger pale-yellow chips with green borders instead of DIGIT’s peach-blue tags. Secondary photo-upload actions also flip from orange outline affordances to green outline affordances. The fifth major divergence is typographic: component internals repeatedly use Inter for action labels and helper text, while DIGIT’s corresponding controls still lean on Roboto or Roboto Condensed.

Destructive recommendations: remove the DIGIT primary CTA contract and replace it with a Nairobi-only button family; replace the shared citizen header with a dedicated `CitizenMobileHeader`; split DIGIT’s generic tag into two Nairobi components, `ComplaintTag` and `OtpCountdownPill`; replace the orange outline upload/add-photo actions with green icon-leading secondary buttons; and replace any generic shadow-card map controls with a dedicated `MapZoomControlStack`. If `digit-ui-esbuild` still carries components such as `LandingPageCard` or generic DIGIT service cards in the complaint journey, they should be removed in favor of Nairobi-specific status/result cards and complaint-flow surfaces instead of being themed in place.
